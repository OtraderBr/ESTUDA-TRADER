So, why trade options on the daily charts?

One obvious answer is traders are
always looking for ways to make money.

Day traders don’t like much overnight
risk, and options allow day traders

to take positions on patterns
that they see on the daily charts

– patterns that might take
several days to reach targets,

and the day traders can take those
positions without much overnight risk.

The absolute risk is smaller than
on the underlying stock.

So if you trade puts or calls
or spreads, what you stand to lose

if the absolute worse happens is always going

to be much less than the underlying stock.

I’m talking about if you’re buying options;

I’m not talking about if you’re selling.

Obviously, if you’re selling
naked puts or naked calls,

you could lose as much
as you can trading stocks.

Also, the cost of execution
and the profit trading options

is about the same as it is for stocks.

Most importantly for
most traders is peace of mind.

The less you have to worry about money,

the more objective
you are about your decisions.

And if you have a big gap against you in
the morning and you have an option position,

it’s going to be much less costly

than if you were holding

So as long as you pick something reasonable

Which option to use,

Monthly options?

Weekly?

How much does a $1 call really cost?

If I pay a dollar for a call,
that call entitles me

to buy 100 shares of stock
at whatever strike price it is.

So let’s say it’s a stock that trades at $50,

and I buy a $50 call
and I pay a dollar for that call.

I’m entitled to buy that call for $50,
even if the stock goes up to $60.

So in other words, I can pay $50
for it even though it’s currently $60,

so I immediately have a $10 profit,
and I paid $1 for the option.

My net profit is $9.

If the call costs $1, that’s $1 per share.

One option, one call,
controls 100 shares of stock.

So if I pay a dollar,
if the price on my computer says

that it’s a dollar for the call,
I’m actually paying $100 for that call.

If I pay a dollar for the call
and sell it for $2.50, I made a $1.50,

but that’s times 100 shares,
so my actual profit is $150.
